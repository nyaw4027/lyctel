1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files—typically containing a unique identifier—that are sent to your browser from a website's computer and stored on your device’s hard drive or mobile memory. They allow the platform to "remember" your actions or preferences over time. This technology is fundamental for nearly all modern web applications to provide essential features like secure logins and personalized settings.

2. Duration Classifications

We use both session and persistent cookies on the Platform, serving distinct operational roles:

Session Cookies

These are temporary files that exist only during your active browsing session. They are automatically deleted the moment you close your browser.

Persistent Cookies

These remain on your device after you close your browser. They are used to recognize you when you return, remembering preferences or keeping you logged in.

3. Why We Use Cookies

The technologies implemented across the Platform generally fall into one of four functional categories:

🔒

Essential (Strictly Necessary)

Critical for Platform security, user authentication, and core functionality (e.g., maintaining your shopping basket).

📈

Performance & Analytics

Collect anonymous data on how visitors interact with the Platform, helping us improve speed and user experience.

🛠️

Functional & Personalization

Remember choices you make (like language or region) to provide enhanced, more personal features.

🎯

Targeting (Not Used for Ads)

Used by integrated partners to facilitate social media sharing or maps; Lynctel does not share data with ad networks.

4. Integrated Third-Party Cookies

To provide specific advanced features (like integrated payment gateways, interactive maps for delivery tracking, or social media sharing widgets), we utilize trusted third-party services. These partners may place their own cookies on your device via our Platform. Since we do not control these technologies, we recommend reviewing their respective privacy and cookies policies.

5. Managing Your Consent & Settings

Your browser or device provides tools to block, delete, or manage cookies. Lynctel respects these settings; however, disabling Essential cookies will cause critical Platform features (like secure login or checkout) to fail.

Most browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can typically modify your setting to decline them. Consult your browser's "Help" section for specific instructions. For cross-browser management across Ghana's primary networks, we suggest starting with these configurations:

Chrome Settings Firefox Options Safari Preferences Edge Configuration
